PRODUCTIVITY RELATED MATTERS

Mom, I am working on the "cure for cancer"

When I was a kid, I loved the nature that San Diego has provided me. The mountains, beaches, deserts, and other natural wonders led me to study marine biology during my undergraduate days at UC San Diego. Ultimately, the calling to do more for society led me to a substance addiction lab and heart tissue lab during my undergraduate. After graduating, I worked at a contract research organization, investigating novel anti-tumor pharmaceuticals. This eventually led me to UC San Diego's Moore's Cancer Center, where I worked at two different labs investigating more novel compounds for anti-tumor activity, and now I run daily lab operations for Dr. Adam Burgoyne's lab.

So why am I here at MET working on my MSSD? Academic labs aren't simply stuck in the realm of bench work and vivariums. As various labs seek to gain the upper-edge in publications, bioinformatics has become hot. I can talk on and on about this, but long story short, there is a demand for people with development and software engineering abilities, and I am here to meet that need.
